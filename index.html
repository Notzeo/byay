On your 20th Birthday 🎂


Dear Bubble,


Happy 20th birthday, dear! 🫧

It is late, and I am sitting here just thinking. Not doing much else. Just thinking about you, about the fact that today you turn 20, and about how much has happened in what is honestly not that long a time.
I want to write this properly. So I am going to take my time with it.
It started on 27 April with just a “hello.”
That sounds simple when I say it like that, but I sat with my phone for a while before I actually sent it. I was nervous in that quiet way where you keep telling yourself it is not a big deal, but your hands are doing something different. I just wanted to talk to you. I did not have a plan beyond that. I just felt like I wanted to know you, and the only way to find out where that could go was to actually say something.
So I did.
And you replied. And then we just started talking.
I think about that sometimes. How much can begin from one small decision to not talk yourself out of something.
The conversations grew slowly, then faster. I found myself looking forward to them in a way that was becoming harder to ignore. There was something natural about talking to you that I was not expecting. Not effortless, but comfortable. Like I did not have to adjust myself to be understood. I could just say what I meant and trust it would land the right way.
By 17 May, I already knew how I felt. I knew it was fast. I was aware of that. But I also did not want to sit on something real and pretend it was not there. So I said it. I told you what I felt and left the rest to you. No pressure. No expectations. Just honesty.
You took your time, which made sense. And then on 24 June, you said yes.
I remember that day clearly.
We met for the first time on 7 August. I tried not to overthink it beforehand, which is its own kind of overthinking. But when it actually happened, it felt easier than I imagined. You were exactly who I had been talking to all those months. That might sound obvious, but it is not always obvious until you are standing in front of someone. With you, it just felt right. Like the person I already knew had simply stepped into real life.
It has been less than a year. Less than a year since that first hello. And when I sit with all of it, it does not feel small. It feels full. It feels like real time, not something passing by unnoticed.
I call you Bubble, and I should explain that properly at least once.
It is not dramatic. It is just that talking to you genuinely makes things feel lighter. There is something about your presence that takes the weight out of ordinary moments. Conversations with you do not feel heavy. They feel easy in a way I do not always know how to describe. So Bubble. It fits.
There are things about you that I notice, and I do not always say them out loud, so let me say some of them here.
Your smile. I am not going to over describe it. I will just say it changes the mood of whatever moment it appears in. It makes things warmer.
The way you are curious about things. You ask questions because you actually want to understand. You go a little deeper instead of staying at the surface. That is something I genuinely admire.
The way you explain things when you are interested in them. You do not try to impress. You just share. And it makes me want to keep listening.
And then there is the part where you sometimes do not see yourself clearly. Not in a dramatic way. Just in small moments where you doubt whether you are ready or capable enough for something. From where I am standing, that doubt does not match reality. You are more capable than you think. You handle more than you give yourself credit for. That is not me trying to be nice. That is just what I see when I pay attention.
You do not have to fully believe that yet. Just keep it somewhere in the back of your mind.
There is something I also want to say honestly.
The past few months have not always been smooth on my side. There were times when I was not in the best headspace because of things going on in my life. And sometimes, without meaning to, I let that affect the way I spoke or reacted. I overthought. I assumed things that were not there. I deleted messages instead of just communicating properly. I said things like maybe you did not want to talk, when that was really just my own insecurity speaking.
If that ever made you feel confused, pressured, or hurt, I am genuinely sorry.
You did not deserve that.
I am learning. Slowly. And I want to be better at handling my emotions instead of letting them spill onto you.
You are 20 today.
That feels important in a quiet way. Not because a number changes everything overnight, but because you are stepping into a phase where things start becoming more real. Decisions feel more yours. The direction you choose starts to shape something solid.
The next few years are going to matter. Not in a dramatic way. Just in the steady way that growth matters.
What I want for you is simple, but it means a lot.
I want you to trust your instincts more. They are better than you think.
I want you to keep being curious, because that curiosity is going to open doors you cannot even see yet.
I want you to build things you are genuinely proud of. Not things that look impressive to other people, but things that feel right to you when you sit with them quietly.
I want you to find clarity about where you want to go, even if it comes slowly.
I want your days to feel steady. I want you to experience progress that makes you smile to yourself. I want you to feel confident in the space you take up instead of questioning whether you deserve it.
And I want you, at some point in your twenties, to look at yourself and realize you were stronger and more capable all along.
Less than a year ago, I was sitting with my phone deciding whether to send one word to someone I barely knew.

I am glad I sent it.
And I am glad it was you on the other side of that hello.

Happy 20th, Bubble.
I hope this year feels like something you can look back on and quietly smile about.


Always here,
Your Person 💖
